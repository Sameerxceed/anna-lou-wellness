# How to read this document
Every page and every email below is shown as a wireframe with the exact text Sameer types in. Sameer should not need to look anywhere else for copy. Where a block contains italic text in quotes, that is the editorial copy as it appears on the page. Where a block contains bullet lists, those are the bullets as written.

Email platform routing.
Two platforms send emails on this site. Every email below is tagged so Sameer knows where it lives.

[TABLE]
FLODESK | All branded welcome sequences and marketing emails. Anna manages templates and content herself inside Flodesk. Triggered by Stripe webhooks via Zapier or direct API.
SYSTEM | Platform-generated receipts and confirmations. Stripe sends the payment receipt automatically. Calendly sends the booking confirmation automatically. Sameer ensures these are branded with the wordmark.
[/TABLE]


Block labels.

[TABLE]
HERO | The first block on the page or in the email.
BODY | Editorial copy. Italic quotes = the literal text on the page.
IMAGE | Photograph placement.
VIDEO | Where Anna's recorded video sits. See video map at the end.
GRID | Multi-card layout.
CTA | Button text + destination.
STRIPE | Stripe checkout trigger.
FORM | Email capture or intake form.
NAV | Top navigation on every page.
FOOT | Footer on every page or email.
META | URL, SEO title, SEO meta, trigger info.
[/TABLE]

The Reset Room is restructured on a three-pillar model, inspired by the structure used by Nikki Meyers and Bella Solanot at OOOM (ooomies.com / The Oasis). The model works because it gives the member three clear, tangible things they get for their monthly fee, and it delivers most of the experience through the place they already are: their podcast player.

Anna does not have a Kajabi account and does not need one. The full Reset Room can be built on the existing stack (ReactJS frontend, Strapi CMS, Stripe subscriptions) with two additional integrations: Hello Audio for the private podcast feed, and Zoom for the monthly live call. Detailed setup follows in Section 3.

## The signup flow, end to end
This is exactly what happens, from the moment someone clicks 'Join the Reset Room':

Step 1.  User clicks 'Join the Reset Room' on the Reset Room landing page. Routed to Stripe Checkout for £25/month subscription.
Step 2.  Stripe processes the payment. Webhook fires to the Strapi backend.
Step 3.  Strapi creates the member account (or activates an existing user) and stamps the role 'reset-room-member'. Generates a unique member ID.
Step 4.  Strapi sends the member ID to Hello Audio via API. Hello Audio returns a unique private RSS URL for that member.
Step 5.  Strapi triggers Resend to send Email 6.1 (Welcome). That email includes: the member portal link, the private podcast RSS URL with subscribe buttons for Apple / Spotify / Overcast, the link to the welcome video (Anna's 90-second intro), and the date of the next Reset Call.
Step 6.  Member is auto-redirected from Stripe Checkout back to the member portal dashboard (/community/reset-room/dashboard), already logged in.


## Cancellation flow
Step 1.  Member clicks 'Manage subscription' in the portal. Routed to Stripe customer portal.
Step 2.  On cancellation, Stripe webhook fires. Strapi removes the 'reset-room-member' role at the end of the current billing period (not immediately, so they keep access through what they have paid for).
Step 3.  Hello Audio is notified via API. The member's private RSS URL is deactivated at period end.
Step 4.  Resend triggers a short 'we're sorry to see you go' email with one optional question (why are you leaving) and a clean unsubscribe.
